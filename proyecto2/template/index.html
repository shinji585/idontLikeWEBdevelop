SYNOPSIS

In a future where climate change has submerged coastal cities, a childlike android programmed with the ability to love embarks on a quest to become real after he is abandoned by his human family. His journey takes him through a dark and futuristic society, where he befriends other 'mechas' and navigates the complex line between human and machine.

Kubrick's Vision

Originally conceived by Stanley Kubrick, who spent years developing the project before his death. The film retains his thematic depth and challenging questions about consciousness and humanity.

Spielberg's Execution

Spielberg brought his signature emotional core and filmmaking craft, creating a unique blend of Kubrick's cerebral concepts with heartfelt storytelling that resonates deeply.

CRITICAL REFLECTION

"Spielberg's A.I. raises profound questions about consciousness, identity, and the nature of love. The film challenges viewers to reconsider what separates humanity from artificial intelligence, forcing an uncomfortable look at our own capacity for both affection and cruelty."

Building on Stanley Kubrick's original vision and Brian Aldiss's short story, the film explores the unsettling possibility of creating beings that can love more purely than their creators. It's a poignant, often melancholic, fairy tale for the technological age that questions the very essence of what makes us human.
